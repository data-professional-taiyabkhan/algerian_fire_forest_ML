Welcome to the Forest Fire Prediction System

Analyze and predict the risk of forest fires based on environmental conditions.

About the Dataset

The Algerian Forest Fire dataset contains environmental and meteorological data from two regions in Algeria: Bejaia and Sidi Bel-abbes. The dataset is used to predict whether a fire is likely to occur under given conditions.

Variables Explained:

  • FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture Code): Measures dryness of fine fuels. Higher values mean increased fire risk.
  • DMC (Duff Moisture Code): Indicates moisture in loose soil layers. Higher values suggest dry conditions.
  • DC (Drought Code): Reflects prolonged dryness in deep soil layers.
  • ISI (Initial Spread Index): Predicts fire spread rate based on wind and fuel dryness.
  • Temperature (Â°C): Higher temperatures dry vegetation, increasing fire risk.
  • RH (Relative Humidity): Low humidity signifies drier air, increasing fire likelihood.
  • Wind (km/h): Strong winds intensify and spread fires.
  • Rain (mm): Rainfall reduces fire risk by moistening vegetation and soil.
  • Region: Geographical area (Bejaia or Sidi Bel-abbes).
  • Fire Occurrence: The model predicts "Yes" or "No" for fire occurrence.
